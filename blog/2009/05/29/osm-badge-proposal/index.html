OSM Badge Proposal

May 29th, 2009

So one of the topics that was prevelant at Where 2.0 and WhereCamp last week was how to get more people mapping for OpenStreetMap.  I’ve been thinking about this topic quite a bit lately and my conclusion is some healthy competition might keep people interested.  Introducting competition has the potential to encourage people to map, but potentially could introduce problems.  The first question that comes to mind for me is will people try to cheat?

If you google €œcheating games€ many of the top searches are cheat codes for video games.  Online games frequently have problems with people cheating  one of the incentives is actual money can be made in online games such as Second Life and World of Warcraft.  Hopefully people would not try to cheat for OpenStreetMap glory, but some people do have an extremely competitive nature.  If prizes other than bragging rights were available then verification of the data would be even more vital.

I think the simplest competition would be to rank people based on distance of GPS traces a contributer uploads.  People would earn badges based on the distances.  Originally I thought about naming the badges after famous geographers, but I think that is too abstract for contributers to OpenStreetMap.  Since contributers are not necessarily from the geospatial field that convention did not necessarily seem appropriate.  I think the best badges would be based on distance and actual uploads.

Below is my proposed badge system:

-Track upload badges

  • < 1 km €œWent to the store€

  • 1-20 km €œTrip to moms€

  • 200 km €œWeekend get away€

  • 320 km €œNew York to Boston€

  • 1100 km €œLondon to Berlin€

  • 5847 km €œParis to New York€

  • 40000 km €œAround the world€

-Contribution Badges

  • 1 upload €œnew mapper€

  • 10 uploads €œoccasional mapper€

  • 100 uploads €œcontributing mapper€

  • 1000 uploads €œserious mapper

  • 10000 uploads €œmap loser€

The above system is just a start, I think that my samples are not enough to encourage people to contribute, since some of the difficult badges could potentially take a lifetime to earn.  Also the 10,000 contribution level would not need to be named after my blog.  I think a combination of incremental easy to earn badges and difficult ones is ideal.  Digital badges could be created for people to display in their blogs and social networking pages.  It would encourage people to advertise for the OpenStreetMap project and hopefully encourage people to continue to contribute.  I plan to flesh out a potential tagged feature system in coming weeks in another entry.
