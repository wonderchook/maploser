Talk: Humanitarian OpenStreetMap Team in Haiti

Nov 18th, 2010

Back in the beginning of October I attended the International Conference on Crisis Mapping. The first day of the event was open to the public and included many ignite talks on a variety of topics related to crisis response. I gave a talk titled “Humanitarian OpenStreetMap Team in Haiti: from Crisis to Capacity Building.” I highlighted how HOT has been working to transfer the maintenance of the Haiti portion of the map to Haiti as well as some ways the data is currently being used.
